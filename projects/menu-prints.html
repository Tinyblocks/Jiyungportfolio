Menu Prints

2023 · Client

This series explores the intersection of traditional Korean aesthetics and contemporary digital art practices. Through a careful study of color relationships and organic forms, each piece investigates the tension between handmade textures and digital precision. The work draws inspiration from traditional Korean pottery glazes, particularly the subtle variations found in celadon ceramics, while incorporating modern screen-printing techniques and digital color manipulation. The project was developed over six months, with each composition representing a different seasonal transition. The layered approach to color application creates depth and movement, inviting viewers to discover new details with prolonged observation. These pieces were originally created for a group exhibition exploring cultural heritage in digital media.
